Transcription, turning audio into text, is one of the few work-from-home jobs you can genuinely start with no experience and no fees. The pay is modest at the start, but the work is real. Here are the honest numbers.

## The real pay ranges

Transcription is usually paid per audio minute, which matters because a one-hour file can take three to four hours to transcribe at first.

- **Beginners on general platforms:** about $0.30 to $0.60 per audio minute, which often works out to **$6 to $16 per hour** of actual work while you are learning.
- **As you get faster:** $15 to $30+ per hour as your speed improves.
- **Specialized (medical, legal):** $20 to $30+ per hour, but these need training or certification.

Honest expectation: many beginners add a few hundred dollars a month, not a full income, until their speed climbs. Anyone advertising "$50/hour transcribing with no experience" is stretching the truth.

## Legit platforms that do not charge you

- **GoTranscript** and **Rev** accept beginners worldwide after a short entrance test. No fee to join.
- **GoTranscript** pays up to about $0.60 per audio minute.
- **Rev** captioners and transcribers earn roughly $0.45 to $1.10 per audio minute.

The rule is simple: a real transcription job pays you and never charges a "starter kit" or "training" fee. If one does, it is the [number-one scam signal](/blog/work-from-home-scam-red-flags).

## What you actually need

- A quiet space and decent headphones.
- Reliable internet and a steady typing speed (40+ words per minute helps a lot).
- Patience while your speed builds. Speed is the whole game in transcription pay.

## How long until your first paycheck

Once you pass a platform's entrance test, you can usually start claiming files **the same week**. Payouts are typically weekly. Your hourly rate is low at first and rises as you learn the formatting and get faster.

## Is it worth it?

It depends on your goal. As a flexible, no-cost, no-experience way to start earning from home, transcription is honest and real. As a path to a full-time income, it is slower than [virtual assistant](/blog/how-much-do-virtual-assistants-make) or freelance writing work unless you specialize.
