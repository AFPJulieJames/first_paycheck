Short answer: **Alignerr is legit — it is a real platform, powered by Labelbox, that pays contributors for AI-training and evaluation work.** It is not a scam. Reviews so far are generally positive on work quality and payment, and for skilled people it is one of the newer, better-regarded options. The usual catch applies: task availability comes and goes.

## What Alignerr is

Alignerr is an AI-training platform run by Labelbox, an established data-labeling company. It hires independent contractors (often called "experts") to help train and evaluate AI models: writing and ranking model responses, checking accuracy and reasoning, coding tasks, and subject-matter evaluation. The work leans toward skill, so writers, coders, and specialists tend to find the better-paid projects.

## The honest pay

Pay depends on your skill area and the project, and Alignerr advertises competitive rates for specialized work:

- **General evaluation and writing tasks:** solid entry-level rates for this category.
- **Coding, STEM, and expert domains:** higher rates, in line with the better platforms in the space.
- **Effective rate depends on availability.** As with all of these platforms, time spent on unpaid onboarding and waiting for tasks lowers your real hourly rate.

Contributors generally report being paid reliably and on schedule, which is the key signal for the "is it legit" question.

## Who does best on Alignerr

The people who earn the most tend to have a genuine edge: strong writing, a coding or STEM background, advanced degrees, or fluency in an in-demand language. Passing the qualification steps honestly unlocks the better-paid projects. Generalists can still participate but compete for the lower-paid, higher-churn work.

## Is Alignerr a scam? No, but watch for impersonators

Alignerr itself is a real, Labelbox-backed platform that pays for completed work. The scam risk, as everywhere in this niche, is **impersonation**: fake recruiters on WhatsApp or Telegram, or lookalike sign-up pages asking for a fee or a [check deposit](/blog/fake-check-job-scams). The genuine platform never charges you to join and never asks you to deposit a check and send money back. If a message does either, it is not Alignerr — check it with the [Scam Smell Test](/#scam).

## The real downsides

- **Availability swings.** Work can be plentiful one week and quiet the next.
- **Onboarding is unpaid.** You invest time in assessments before you know your task volume.
- **It is newer,** so long-term, large-sample word-of-mouth is still building compared with older platforms.

## The verdict

Alignerr is legitimate and one of the more promising newer AI-training platforms in 2026, especially for people with writing or technical skills.
