Bookkeeping is one of the most stable work-from-home paths, and you do not need to be a CPA to start. Here is what beginners actually earn, and how to begin without paying for an expensive course.

## Honest pay for beginners

- **Starting rate:** about $25 to $40 an hour.
- **Experienced:** $40 to $60+ an hour with a full client roster.
- **Part-time monthly:** roughly $1,000 to $3,000 once you have a few recurring clients.

Bookkeeping pays steadily rather than spectacularly, and that is the appeal: monthly clients who stay for years.

## How long until your first client

Plan for **one to three months** to land your first paying client. It is a bit slower to start than virtual assistant work, but stickier once you are going. If you need money this week, see [faster on-ramps here](/blog/work-from-home-jobs-no-experience).

## How to start without an expensive course

You do not need a $2,000 "bookkeeping certification" that promises guaranteed clients. Start free:

1. Learn the basics with **free QuickBooks or Xero training**.
2. Practice categorizing a sample month of transactions.
3. Offer to do the books for one small business you know, even at a starter rate.
4. Get comfortable with the monthly close and a simple profit-and-loss report.
5. Ask that first client for a testimonial and a referral.

> Skip any program that promises guaranteed income or charges thousands upfront. You can start with free software and one real client.

## Where the clients are

Your first client is often someone you already know. Beyond that: QuickBooks Live, Belay, and Upwork for building a track record.
