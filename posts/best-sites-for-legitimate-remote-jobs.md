There is no shortage of "remote job" sites; there is a shortage of ones that are actually worth your time and not full of scams. Here are the legit places to look in 2026, and how to tell a real job board from a trap.

## The vetted, legit boards

None of these charge you to apply (a couple offer optional paid memberships, but applying is free):

- **The Mom Project** and **HireMyMom**: flexible, family-friendly roles. ([Is The Mom Project legit?](/blog/is-the-mom-project-legit))
- **FlexJobs**: hand-screened remote listings (paid membership, scam-free by design).
- **We Work Remotely** and **Remote OK**: large remote boards, strong for tech and operations.
- **LinkedIn and Indeed**: filter for "remote"; huge volume, just vet each posting.
- **Company career pages**: the safest of all. Find a company you like and apply directly.

## For building your own income (not just a job)

If you want client work rather than a single employer, platforms like **Upwork** (high volume) and niche boards like **ProBlogger** for writers or **QuickBooks Live** for bookkeepers are where you find gigs. More in [legit work-from-home jobs with no experience](/blog/work-from-home-jobs-no-experience).

## How to spot a fake "job site"

- It charges a fee to see listings or to "get matched." Real boards do not.
- Listings promise high pay for no skill and no interview.
- It pushes you to a personal DM, WhatsApp, or Telegram to "apply."
- It asks for bank details or a "starter kit" before any offer.

> A real job pays you. Any job site that asks you to pay it is the scam.

## Frequently asked questions

### What are the best sites to find legitimate remote jobs?
Vetted, legit options include FlexJobs (hand-screened), We Work Remotely and Remote OK (large remote boards), LinkedIn and Indeed (filter for "remote"), and family-friendly boards like The Mom Project and HireMyMom. The safest of all is applying directly on a company's career page.

### Do legit remote job sites charge a fee?
Applying should always be free. A couple of boards (like FlexJobs) offer an optional paid membership for screened listings, but any site that charges a fee just to see listings or to "get matched" is a scam.

### Where do I find remote client work instead of a single employer?
For gig and client work, platforms like Upwork (high volume) and niche boards like ProBlogger for writers or QuickBooks Live for bookkeepers are where the work is.

### How do I spot a fake job site?
Watch for sites that charge to see listings or get matched, promise high pay for no skill and no interview, push you to a personal DM or WhatsApp to "apply," or ask for bank details or a starter kit before any offer. A real job pays you; any job site that asks you to pay it is the scam.
