Short-form video is everywhere, and the people creating it cannot keep up with the editing. That backlog is your opportunity. Video editing is one of the most in-demand remote skills in 2026, and you can start with a laptop and free software. Here is the honest path, including what it really pays at the start.

## What the work actually is

Most beginner video editing is not Hollywood. It is:

- Cutting long footage into tight short-form clips for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts
- Adding captions, simple graphics, music, and basic transitions
- Trimming podcasts or interviews into highlight clips
- Cleaning up talking-head videos for coaches, course creators, and small brands

The demand is heaviest in short-form social content, which is also the easiest to learn. You do not need cinematic skills to do this well, you need to be fast, consistent, and good at keeping a viewer's attention.

## What beginners actually earn

Honest 2026 numbers. Entry-level remote video editing generally runs:

- **$15 to $25 an hour** when you are truly starting out
- **$21 to $40 an hour** as you build a portfolio and speed
- **Per-project rates** are common for short-form: a simple edited short might pay $15 to $50 depending on length and client

Anyone advertising "$100k as a video editor in 90 days with no experience" is selling a course. The real path is steadier and absolutely reachable.

## How to start with no experience

1. **Pick free software.** CapCut and DaVinci Resolve are free and used professionally. You do not need to buy anything to begin.
2. **Learn one style well.** Short-form vertical video with captions is the highest-demand, lowest-barrier place to start.
3. **Build a reel by editing real content.** Take public footage or volunteer to edit a few videos for a small creator. Three or four strong sample edits beat any certificate.
4. **Post your edits where clients look.** A simple portfolio, plus posting your edits on the same platforms you want to work in, gets you noticed.
5. **Pitch creators who post a lot but edit poorly.** Coaches, local businesses, and growing creators are drowning in raw footage. Offer to edit a free sample, then convert them to a paying rate.
6. **Charge per video or per package** once you have proof, and raise rates as your turnaround speeds up.

## Why this skill compounds

Editing gets faster the more you do it, which means your effective hourly rate climbs even if your per-project price stays the same. A short that took you two hours at the start takes thirty minutes after a few months. Speed plus a small portfolio is how editors go from $15 an hour to genuinely good money.

## Avoid the traps

You will run into fake clients and "agencies." No legitimate client sends you a check before you have worked and asks you to send some back, that is the [fake-check scam](/blog/fake-check-job-scams). And you never need to pay to join an "editing network." If an offer feels off, the [Scam Smell Test](/#scam) takes about a minute.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can I get a video editing job with no experience?

Yes. Clients hire on portfolio, not credentials. Build three or four strong sample edits using free software, focus on short-form vertical video, and pitch creators who post often but edit poorly.

### What software do beginner video editors use?

CapCut and DaVinci Resolve are both free and used by professionals. You do not need to buy expensive software to start earning.

### How much do beginner video editors make?

Realistically $15 to $25 an hour starting out, rising to $21 to $40 an hour with a portfolio and faster turnaround. Short-form edits are often priced per project, around $15 to $50 each.

### What kind of video editing is easiest to start with?

Short-form vertical clips for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts. They are in the highest demand and require the least advanced skill, mostly cutting, captions, and pacing.
