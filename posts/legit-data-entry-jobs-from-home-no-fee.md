"Data entry from home" is one of the most searched work-from-home terms, which means it is also one of the most scammed. Real data entry jobs exist and pay modestly. The "easy $30/hour typing" ads almost never do. Here is how to find the real ones.

## What honest data entry pays

Legitimate remote data entry usually pays about **$12 to $18 an hour**. It is steady, low-barrier work, not a high earner. Anyone promising far more for "no experience, just typing" is baiting you.

## Where to look (no fee, ever)

Stick to real job boards and company career pages, not DMs or ads:

- General job boards filtered for remote (Indeed, LinkedIn) under "data entry."
- Vetted-remote boards like FlexJobs.
- Established companies that hire remote clerical and operations staff.

You should never pay for access, a "starter kit," training, or certification to do data entry. That fee is the scam.

## The scams that target this exact search

- **Starter-kit fees.** "Pay $39 for your data-entry kit." Real employers send you work, not invoices.
- **Check overpayment.** They mail a check, you deposit it, then "accidentally overpaid, wire some back." The check bounces and you are liable. More in [work-from-home scam red flags](/blog/work-from-home-scam-red-flags).
- **Too-good pay.** "$30/hour, no experience, work whenever." If it sounds amazing and asks nothing of you, it is bait.

> A real job pays you. If a data-entry "job" asks you to pay anything to begin, walk away.

## A better-paying alternative

If you can type and stay organized, **virtual assistant work** pays more ($18 to $35 an hour) for similar skills and is just as beginner-friendly. See [how to become a virtual assistant with no experience](/blog/how-to-become-a-virtual-assistant-with-no-experience).
