Short answer: **ModSquad is legit, it is an established company offering real remote moderation and customer-support gigs, but pay is modest and you work as a contractor with no benefits.** It is a genuine option in the trust-and-safety and support space. Here is the honest 2026 rundown.

## What ModSquad is

ModSquad (the "Mods") provides outsourced **community moderation, customer support, and social media management** for brands. It hires remote independent contractors ("Mods") to staff these gigs. The company is well known in the content-moderation world and has been around for years, so it is clearly legitimate, not a scam.

## What it really pays

Pay is fair-to-modest for the field:

- **Content moderation:** roughly **$18 to $26 an hour** per recent reviews.
- **Customer support gigs:** often lower, around **$15 to $19 an hour**.

You are an **independent contractor**, so there are no benefits, hours can vary by project, and you handle your own taxes. Glassdoor reviews average around 3.0 out of 5, with the common refrain being decent flexibility but low pay and limited worker protections that come with contractor status.

## The honest catches

- **Contractor, not employee:** no benefits, variable hours, project-dependent work.
- **Project-based:** gigs can start and end, so income is not always continuous.
- **Moderation can be heavy:** trust-and-safety work sometimes means reviewing unpleasant content. Know yourself before signing up for that.

None of this is fraud, it is just the honest shape of the work.

## Is it safe? Yes

ModSquad pays its contractors and charges nothing to join, so it passes our scam checks. As always, the real risk is impersonation, a fake "ModSquad recruiter" running the [fake-check scam](/blog/fake-check-job-scams). The genuine company never asks you to pay or to deposit a check and send money back. Verify offers through the official site and sanity-check anything odd with the [Scam Smell Test](/#scam).

## Frequently asked questions

### Is ModSquad a scam?

No. ModSquad is a legitimate, established company offering real remote moderation and support gigs. The drawbacks are modest pay and contractor status, not fraud.

### How much does ModSquad pay?

Content moderation gigs commonly pay about $18 to $26 an hour, while customer-support gigs run lower, around $15 to $19. You are paid as an independent contractor with no benefits.

### Is ModSquad steady work?

Not always. Gigs are project-based, so hours and availability vary, and income can be inconsistent between projects.

### Does ModSquad charge a fee to join?

No. ModSquad never charges you to start. Any "recruiter" asking for payment or sending a check to deposit and partly return is an impersonation scam.
