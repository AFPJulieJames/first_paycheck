Short answer: **Working Solutions is legit and one of the better-regarded remote customer-service companies, though you work as an independent contractor and pay varies by program.** It is a real, BBB-listed company with generally positive worker reviews. Here is the honest 2026 picture.

## What Working Solutions is

Working Solutions hires remote **independent contractors** for customer-service, sales, and tech-support programs across industries (travel, retail, healthcare, and more). It has been around for decades and is well established, so legitimacy is not in question. Importantly, Working Solutions does **not charge** you to apply or work, which already sets it apart from pay-to-start models.

## What it really pays

Pay depends on the program and is often performance-influenced. Reported rates commonly land in the **$10 to $30 an hour** range depending on the contract, with some programs advertising around $30/hour for the right work. As a contractor you have no benefits and handle your own taxes, but the upside is flexible scheduling and legitimate, named-client work. Glassdoor reviews average around **3.6 out of 5**, with about 71% of workers saying they would recommend it, which is strong for this category.

## The honest catches

- **Independent contractor:** no benefits, your own taxes, hours that vary by program.
- **Pay varies a lot:** some programs pay well, others modestly, and some include performance pay.
- **Onboarding can take time:** applications, screening, and unpaid program training are normal here.

These are ordinary trade-offs of contractor support work, not red flags.

## Is it safe? Yes

Working Solutions pays its contractors and never charges a fee, so it passes our scam checks cleanly. The company even publishes its own work-from-home scam warnings. The real risk is impersonation, a fake "Working Solutions recruiter" running the [fake-check scam](/blog/fake-check-job-scams). The genuine company never sends you a check to deposit and return, or asks you to pay. Verify through the official jobs.workingsolutions.com site and check anything odd with the [Scam Smell Test](/#scam).

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Working Solutions a scam?

No. Working Solutions is a legitimate, long-established remote customer-service company with generally positive reviews and no fees to join. The trade-offs are contractor status and variable pay, not fraud.

### How much does Working Solutions pay?

Pay varies by program, commonly in the $10 to $30 an hour range, with some contracts advertising around $30/hour and some including performance pay. You are paid as an independent contractor.

### Does Working Solutions charge a fee?

No. It never charges you to apply or work. Any "recruiter" requesting payment or sending a check to deposit and return is an impersonation scam, not the real company.

### Is Working Solutions a good company to work for?

For this category, yes. It scores around 3.6 out of 5 with roughly 71% of workers recommending it, which is strong. Just go in expecting contractor status and program-dependent pay.
