Short answer: **the companies below are all legit — real, non-scam platforms that genuinely pay** — but being legitimate is not the same as being worth your time. Some pay reasonably; others are "real, but barely worth it." This page is the honest index: what each one is, how we rate it, and the catch, with a link to the full review. Our one rule never changes: a real job pays you, you never pay it. If a "recruiter" for any of these asks for money or sends you a check to deposit, that's an impersonation scam — check it with the [Scam Smell Test](/#scam).

## How we judge "legit"

We are not affiliates and earn no commission on any company here, so there's no reason to inflate a verdict. - **[Working Solutions](/blog/is-working-solutions-legit)** (★4) — One of the better-regarded remote customer-service companies; you work as an independent contractor and pay varies by program.

## Frequently asked questions

### Are these work-from-home companies legit or scams?

All the companies reviewed here are legitimate — they are real platforms that pay their workers with no fee to join. The honest caveat is that "legit" doesn't mean "great pay." Several pay modestly or offer inconsistent work, which is why each review separates whether a company is real from whether it's worth your time.

### Which pays the best?

Among these, the higher-rated platforms — DataAnnotation.tech, Outlier AI, Alignerr (AI training), and Working Solutions (customer service) — tend to pay most reliably, though earnings still depend on task availability, your speed, and the specific program. Read each review for honest pay ranges.

### How do I know if a "recruiter" from one of these is real?

Real companies never ask you to pay a fee or deposit a check and send money back. If a message claiming to be from any of these platforms does that, it's an impersonation scam, not the real company. Paste it into the free Scam Smell Test to check the red flags in seconds.
