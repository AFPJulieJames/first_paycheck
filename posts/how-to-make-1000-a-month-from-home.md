An extra $1,000 a month is one of the most realistic and life-changing goals in work-from-home, and it does not require a "secret system." Here is an honest plan for how to actually get there.

## What $1,000 a month really takes

At a normal starting rate, $1,000 a month is roughly **8 to 12 hours a week**. That is the honest trade: a real part-time effort, not "20 minutes a day from your phone." The good news is that it is very achievable on several legit paths within a couple of months.

## The fastest paths to $1,000/month

- **Virtual assistant** ($18 to $35/hr): about 8 to 14 hours a week gets you there. Fastest to start. See [how to become a VA with no experience](/blog/how-to-become-a-virtual-assistant-with-no-experience).
- **Bookkeeping** ($25 to $50/hr): one or two monthly clients can hit $1,000. Slower to land, very stable. See [how much beginner bookkeepers make](/blog/how-much-do-beginner-bookkeepers-make).
- **Freelance writing** ($25 to $75/hr): a few regular articles a month. More uneven at the start.

## The honest timeline

- **Weeks 1 to 4:** set up, pitch, land your first client. Often little or no income yet. This is normal.
- **Months 2 to 3:** first steady client, partial income.
- **Months 3 to 6:** a second client and you are at or past $1,000.

Anyone promising $1,000 this week is selling something. Real income ramps.

## What will not get you there

Surveys, "data entry" ad-bait, and most trending fads. The math does not work. If a path promises big money for no skill and no time, it is bait. ([Are online surveys worth it?](/blog/are-online-surveys-worth-it))
